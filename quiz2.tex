\begin{exercise}
Let $\{a_n\}$ be a bounded (not necessarily convergent) sequence, and assume $\lim\limits_{n\to \infty} b_n = 0$.  Show that $\lim\limits_{n\to\infty} a_nb_n=0$.  Why are we not allowed to use the Algebraic Limit Theorem to prove this? 
\end{exercise}
\newpage
\begin{exercise}
Recall that the formula for the geometric series is $\sum\limits_{k=0}^\infty ar^k= \frac{a}{1-r}$ for any $|r| < 1$.  Use the fact that geometric series converge to show that 
\begin{align*}
    \sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{2^n + n}
\end{align*}
converges.
\end{exercise}
